10 lessons I learned at Google

Jason Xu
Reflections on Attitude, Collaboration, & Execution
Loosely based on Addy’s presentation

1. The best engineers are obsessed with solving user problems.

2. Eventually, time becomes more valuable than money.

3. Bias towards action. Ship.

4. There are no shortcuts, but there is compounding.

5. Being right is cheap. Getting to right together is the real work.

6. Most “slow” teams are actually misaligned teams.

7. Admitting what you don’t know creates more safety than pretending you do.

8. When a measure becomes a target, it stops measuring.

9. Clarity is seniority. Cleverness is overhead.

10. Think 100x.

Thank You.

Stay curious. Stay humble.
Remember that the work is always about the people.
